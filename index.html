About Me:

Home Town: Pawcatuck, Connecticut

High School: Stonington High School (SHS)

College: Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT)

LinkedIn: Profile

Ever since I was a kid I always went to video games to lose myself and escape from the real world. It gave me a sanctuary to be myself and explore who I could be. Whether I had friends to play with or not, I was almost exclusivly either playing games, or watching videos about them. For as long as I can remember whenever I thought about what I wanted to do, I never thought twice about becoming a Game Designer/Developer.

Fortunatly for me, my high school had some Game Design and Development classes that I took as soon as posible. There I found a new type of enjoyment when it comes to video games, making them. I learned how to make simple games using Game Maker, starting off with drag and drop and moving on to GML (Game Maker Language). While it isn't a popular language, it gave me an idea of what coding would be like.

Unfortunatly my school only offered two levels of GDD classes. I tried to get a GDD independent study but it didn't work out. Alternatly, I took an independent study of Web Development (after taking Web Dev 1) and helped work on my schools website.

Now, I am curently a forth year student at Rochester Institute of Technology and am taking part in its Game Design and Development major. I have a particular passion for game design, but also enjoy coding. I have had the ability to work on multiple games as a game designer from physical to digital, and it's made it clear I'm in the right place.

Backyard

Tools: Unity, Figma

Role: Game, Level, and Co-Narrative Designer

Link: N/A

Languages: C#

Description: Backyard is a game of perspective. Through the years time changes all, and this game explores what that looks likes in regards to a house and it's backyard. By exploring the backyard in different moments in time, through different windows shifting perspectives and solving puzzles, the player learns of a childs life as they grew up. The game consists of three unique puzzles that allow them access throughout the house. This gives them the ability to use more windows, which gives them new perspectives to view the backyard, as well as different times. By exploring and solving the puzzles, the player gains insight into the life of this child and how time impacted their life.
